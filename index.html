About Us

Mary Celeste

Bound by curiosity.
Driven by craft.

Madelena Kishner and Riccardo Di Capua are colleagues bound by a shared love of creativity, curiosity, and the kind of give-and-take intellectual adventure that produces something neither could make alone. Their books range from the Venetian decadence of The Stones Remember — tracing the intertwined fates of Peggy Guggenheim, Luisa Casati, and Gabriele D'Annunzio — to the global sweep of Rooms Where History Broke, fifteen narrative nonfiction stories of the moments when hidden forces changed everything; from the accessible wonder of Materials: The Spectacular Science of Stuff to their most recent work, Artificial Intelligence in Our Daily Lives, which makes the case that the future everyone is waiting for has already arrived.

Kishner brings to the partnership a background in fintech and a gift for atmospheric, character-driven prose that renders historical figures with psychological intimacy; Di Capua brings analytical rigor and a materials scientist's eye for structure, innovation, and the beauty that comes from within.

Together they have lectured and presented on topics spanning the history of science, the nature of artistic obsession, the biography of materials, and the cultural impact of artificial intelligence. Their podcast series — including Rooms Where History Broke, Whispers of the Unseen, and Journey Through Time — extend their narrative work into audio, reaching listeners who want history told the way it actually felt to live it: urgent, strange, and impossible to put down.
